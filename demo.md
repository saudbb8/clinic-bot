# Melbourne Physio AI Receptionist
## Case Study: How One Clinic Saved $2,200/Month and Gave Their Receptionist Her Mornings Back

---

### The Problem — Before

**Collins Street Physiotherapy** (a typical independent Melbourne clinic) had 3 practitioners seeing 60 patients per week.

Their receptionist, Lisa, spent her mornings doing the same three things every single day:

- Calling patients to confirm tomorrow's appointments (45–60 calls)
- Chasing no-shows and last-minute cancellations
- Manually rescheduling patients who couldn't make their slot

**The numbers were painful:**
- Average no-shows per week: **6–8 patients**
- Lost revenue per no-show: **$120**
- Weekly revenue lost to no-shows: **$720–$960**
- Monthly revenue lost: **$2,880–$3,840**
- Hours Lisa spent on phone follow-ups daily: **2.5–3 hours**

Lisa was exhausted. The clinic owner was frustrated. And patients were slipping through the cracks — not because the clinic didn't care, but because one person physically couldn't manage 60 relationships manually every week.

---

### The Solution — Sophie

We built Sophie — an AI receptionist that lives inside the clinic's WhatsApp number.

Sophie handles everything Lisa used to do manually, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

**24 hours before every appointment, Sophie messages each patient:**

> "Hi James! It's Sophie from Melbourne Physio. Just a friendly reminder that you've got an appointment with Dr. Chen tomorrow at 2pm. Does that still work for you? 😊"

**If the patient confirms:**
> Sophie logs it and the receptionist sees a clean confirmed list every morning.

**If the patient needs to reschedule:**
> Sophie handles the entire conversation naturally — finds a new time, confirms it, done. No human involved.

**If the patient doesn't reply:**
> Sophie follows up 3 hours later. If still no reply, Lisa gets a notification to make one targeted call — instead of 60 untargeted ones.

**After the appointment, Sophie checks in:**
> "Hi James, hope your session went well today! How's the back feeling? We'd love to see you again — want me to book your next appointment now while we have you?"
